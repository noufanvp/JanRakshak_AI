India AI Impact Festival
Comprehensive Registration & Submission Procedure Guide
Phase 1: Getting Started
Begin the registration process by navigating to the official website.
Visit the portal at: https://indiaaiimpactfest.ai-for-all.in/
Click on the "Register Now" button on the home page.
You will be prompted to sign in. It is recommended to use "Register with Google" and select your Gmail
account.
• 
• 


Phase 2: Selecting the Category
After logging in, you must select your competing category.
Choose "AI Impact Creators". This category is specifically designed for students studying in K-12, higher
education, or equivalent ecosystems.
• 


Phase 3: Initial Registration (Profile Creation)
Fill in your personal, contact, and educational details to complete your profile.
Individual vs. Group Submissions
For Individual Submissions: Fill out the profile details to create your account, and you can immediately
proceed to the entry submission.
For Group Submissions: Each student in the group must individually follow Phases 1 to 3 to create
their own personal account using their own Gmail and phone number.
• 
• 


Phase 4: Section A — Personal Information & Group Configuration
Once all members are registered, the Team Leader will initiate the submission form.
Group Project: Select "Yes" under "Is this a group project?".
Send Collaborations: Enter the registered email IDs of the other team members and click "Verify
teammate emails".
Action Required by Teammates: The entered teammates will receive a collaboration request on their
respective dashboards. Each student must log into their own account and accept the request
before the final submission can be completed. 
• 
• 


Phase 5: Section B — Project Details (Part 1)
Fill out the technical details of the project adhering to the word limits.
B1. Title: Max 5 words.
B2. Problem Statement: Include evidence/citations. Max 150 words.
B3. Target Audience: Max 100 words.
B4. Your Solution: Max 50 words.
B5. How it works: Max 250 words.
B6-B9: Detail the AI advancements, dataset used, accessibility features, and societal/environmental
impact.

Phase 7: Section C — 120-Second Solution Video
Upload a mandatory visual demonstration (Max 120 seconds, Max 60 MB, MP4/WMV/FLV/AVI). A portion
must show the team on camera. No AI voiceovers. YouTube links not accepted.

Map your project to specific national initiatives.
