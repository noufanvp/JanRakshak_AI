IndiaAI Impact Festival 2026
School Participation Guide, Guidelines & Submission Framework
1. Overview of the IndiaAI Impact Festival
The IndiaAI Impact Festival is a premier national innovation platform designed to 
catalyze artificial intelligence readiness, creativity, and real-world problem-solving 
across India's schools. Aligned with the goals of India's National AI Mission and the 
National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, the festival showcases groundbreaking AI and
AIoT innovations built by young minds and educators with a defined social purpose.
Organized under a collaborative framework involving the Ministry of Electronics and 
Information Technology (MeitY), INDIAai Mission, Software Technology Parks of India
(STPI), National Institute of Electronics and Information Technology (NIELIT), Atal 
Innovation Mission (AIM), NITI Aayog, and Intel India, the initiative acts as an 
accelerator for shifting AI from theoretical code to real-world school-led 
applications.
CORE THEME: The core theme of the festival is 'AI for Bharat: Enriching 
Lives with AI Innovation.' All competitive school entries must present 
responsible, ethical, and community-centric models aligned with the 
United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
2. School Eligibility & Submission Dynamics
Team vs. Individual Submissions
To encourage both focused student ingenuity and collaborative peer problem-
solving, the festival allows flexibility in student configurations:
 Individual Submissions: Eligible for all students and school educators. Ideal 
for highly focused single-developer projects or unique pedagogical 
implementations.
 Team Submissions: Permitted and highly encouraged in the 'Impact Creators' 
(Student) category. The team size must be strictly restricted to a maximum of 
three (3) school students. During submission, the primary applicant must 
explicitly register the contact details and email addresses of all team members 
to guarantee co-certification and accurate school documentation.

Project Types & Technical Mandates
The festival is built specifically around functional technology implementations rather
than purely theoretical text documents. The core project standards include:
 Mandatory AI/AIoT Component: Submissions must integrate functional 
Artificial Intelligence (e.g., computer vision, natural language processing, 
machine learning models) or AIoT (AI integrated with Internet of Things edge 
devices). Projects entirely devoid of operational AI components will be 
automatically disqualified during initial screening.
 Maturity Level: Solutions should range from functional working prototypes to 
school-tested frameworks. Pure ideation or text notes without working code or 
models are not accepted.

Special Tracks & Recognition Sub-Categories
Beyond standard age groups, the panel evaluates school entries under highly 
focused lenses to promote inclusivity:
 AI for Accessibility: A core sub-track dedicated specifically to solutions 
designed to alleviate barriers for neurodivergent or physically differently-abled 
citizens.
 Nari Shakti Special Mention: Awarded to exceptional AI innovations 
engineered entirely by individual female students or all-girl student developer 
teams.
 Aspirational Districts Track: Special recognition for high-impact localized 
systems engineered by talent within designated rural or developing aspirational 
districts.
4. Key Strategic Themes for Student Projects
School participants are expected to design solutions targeting explicit 
socioeconomic sectors. The primary pillars include:
Agriculture & Precision Farming
AI-enabled models built to combat crop yield reduction due to weather 
unpredictability, optimize soil resource management, and provide micro-level 
market pricing insights to local farmers.
Healthcare & MedTech Equity
Applications focusing on mitigating severe medical personnel shortages in rural 
areas, enabling early automated diagnostics through computer vision, and 
streamlining school or community health tracking.
Education, Skilling & The Digital Divide
AI architectures built to deliver personalized curriculum structures at scale, bridge 
deep learning voids for disadvantaged children, and support multi-lingual 
pedagogical tools.
Financial Inclusion & Digital Security
Micro-credit profiling ideas for informal merchants, coupled with robust AI fraud 
prevention layers for UPI and everyday digital transactions.
Wildcard & Open Innovation Track
An unrestricted track welcoming groundbreaking implementations across Safe & 
Trusted AI, Green Energy, Science & DeepTech optimizations, and resilient Urban 
Infrastructure solutions.

5. The Multi-Tier Evaluation Architecture
Submissions undergo a strict multi-tier vetting mechanism to highlight high-
potential school innovations:
Level 1: Primary Structural Vetting & Initial Screening
Conducted by an independent third-party expert assessment firm. All uploaded 
materials (PDF synopses, design documentation, and strict 2-minute media files) are
checked for compliance. The top fifteen (15) projects from each sub-category are 
selected to advance.
Level 2: Expert Mentorship & Engineering Refinement
The remaining top 15 shortlisted school teams are given access to high-impact 
technical bootcamps. Teams undergo intensive virtual and offline mentoring 
sessions led by elite engineering stakeholders from Intel India and premier 
academic centers. This stage allows students to debug models, refine code, and 
optimize their projects before final re-submission.
Level 3: Virtual Jury Presentation & Final Selection
Shortlisted student innovators present operational demos directly via live virtual 
rooms to an elite jury comprising senior government policymakers, technology 
experts, and top academic minds. The evaluation is weighted across:
Evaluation Dimension Core Measurement Focus
Solution Definition Clarity and depth of the targeted 
localized problem statement.
Technical Feasibility Sophistication, stability, and depth of 
the student's underlying AI/AIoT model.
Social & Sustainable Impact Direct alignment with the UN SDGs and 
measurable capacity to improve lives.
Team Competence & Presentation Technical proficiency, clarity, and 
presentation skills during live jury

6. Key School Milestones & National Rewards
Critical Deadlines
The national timeline must be tightly followed by all schools:
 Official Event Launch: June 10, 2026
 Final Date for Online Entry Submission: July 15, 2026
 Jury Deliberation & Winners Announcement: Scheduled across Late Q3 / 
Early Q4 2026.
Incentives, Awards, and Global Pathways
The festival provides substantial resources, high-level pathways, and global 
recognition for top performing schools:
 Global Pathway (Team India Nomination): The top three (3) designated 
school AI Impact Creators will be officially nominated as 'Team India' to 
showcase their technical solutions at the prestigious global level during the Intel 
AI Global Impact Festival 2026.
 National Felicitation & Media Visibility: Winning students, educators, and 
schools will be showcased at national high-level government forums, receive co-
branded government certificates, and secure features in the AI Innovation 
Compendium.
 Incubator & Toolkit Support: Top placing innovations gain access to deep 
mentoring networks and school-level implementation toolkits designed to fast-
track deployment across local communities.
